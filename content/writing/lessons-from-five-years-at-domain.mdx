# Lessons From Five Years at Domain

I spent five and a half years at Domain across four roles. I joined as a frontend developer on Realtime Agent and left as a senior engineer working across Agent Solutions Platforms. Here is what stuck with me.

## Real-time UI is a mindset

Auctions and live offers do not forgive latency. Every design decision — state shape, polling strategy, optimistic updates, error handling — shapes how confident users feel in high-stakes moments.

## Deployment velocity is a product metric

If releasing is hard, teams ship less value. Investing in CI/CD, feature flags, and rollback tooling pays for itself in faster learning and calmer incidents.

## Seniority is multiplier work

The best thing I did was not a pull request. It was helping the team around me make better decisions: cleaner architecture, clearer code review, stronger testing habits, and more honest prioritisation.

Growth in senior engineering is measured in the quality of the systems and people you leave behind.
